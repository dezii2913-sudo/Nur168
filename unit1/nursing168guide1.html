💬 Chapter 8: Communication

Master the art of therapeutic communication in nursing practice

🔄 The Communication Process (Berlo's Model) +
📌 Remember This
Communication is a dynamic, circular process - not just one-way!

🧠 MNEMONIC: "SMCRF" - Some Messages Create Real Feedback

SStimulus
MMessage
CChannel
RReceiver
FFeedback
  • Stimulus: Initiates the communication
  • Sender/Encoder: Person who begins the message
  • Message: The communication product
  • Channel: Auditory, visual, or kinesthetic pathway
  • Receiver/Decoder: Translates and interprets the message
  • Feedback: Confirms the message was received
🗣️ Forms of Communication +

💬 Verbal Communication

Depends on understanding of language between sender and receiver

🤫 Nonverbal Communication

  • Facial expressions & eye contact
  • Gestures & touch
  • Body movements, posture, gait
  • Space & territoriality
  • Physical appearance & dress
  • Sounds (moaning, crying, sighing)
⚠️ Test Alert
Touch is a personal behavior that means different things to different people - it's NOT universal!
📊 Four Levels of Communication +
Level Description Example
Intrapersonal Self-talk; communication within a person "I've got this" before a presentation
Interpersonal Between two or more people to exchange messages Nurse talking with patient
Small Group Communication within a small team Nursing team huddle
Organizational Communication across an organization Hospital-wide memos

💡 Quick Tip

Intrapersonal = Inside yourself (think: intra-venous goes INSIDE the vein)

📋 SBAR Communication Technique +
📌 Definition
SBAR is a standardized hand-off communication technique used to relay patient information clearly and concisely.

S - Situation

What is happening with the patient RIGHT NOW?

B - Background

What is the clinical background/context?

A - Assessment

What do I think the problem is?

R - Recommendation

What do I think should be done?

✅ Patient Safety Goal
SBAR supports Joint Commission National Patient Safety Goals for improving communication!
🤝 Therapeutic Nurse-Patient Relationship +
📌 Key Characteristics
  • Does NOT occur spontaneously - requires intention
  • Characterized by unequal sharing (patient shares more)
  • Built on the PATIENT'S needs
  • Nurse is professionally accountable
  • Purposeful and TIME-LIMITED

Three Phases of the Therapeutic Relationship:

Phase What Happens Key Activity
1. Orientation Establishing the relationship Contract/agreement made about goals, frequency, duration
2. Working Achieving goals Nurse provides assistance, counseling, teaching
3. Termination Ending the relationship Examine goals for attainment

😊 Dispositional Traits

  • Warmth & friendliness

  • Empathy
  • Honesty & authenticity
  • Trust & caring
  • Competence

🔨 Rapport Builders

  • Specific objectives
  • Comfortable environment

❓ Interviewing Techniques +
Technique Purpose
Open-ended Allows patient to express freely; cannot be answered yes/no
Closed Specific, limited response expected
Validating Confirms what nurse heard or observed
Clarifying Makes unclear statements more understandable
Reflective Encourages deeper exploration
Sequencing Establishes order of events
Directing Focuses conversation on specific topic
⚠️ Test Tip
Validating questions confirm what nurse heard - NOT open-ended questions!
💪 Assertive vs Aggressive Behavior +

✅ Assertive (GOOD)

  • Confident, open body posture
  • Clear, concise "I" statements
  • Shares thoughts effectively
  • Works with or without supervision
  • Remains calm under supervision
  • Asks for help when needed
  • Admits mistakes, takes responsibility

❌ Aggressive (BAD)

  • Violates rights of others
  • Can be verbal or physical
  • Marked by tension and anger
  • Angry tone, condescending
  • Threatening behavior
  • Focus on "winning at all costs"
  • Inhibits collaboration
🚫 Blocks to Communication +
⚠️ Avoid These!
  • Failure to perceive patient as human being
  • Failure to listen
  • Using clichés
  • Using "why" and "how" questions (can feel accusatory)
  • Probing for information
  • Using leading questions
  • Giving advice
  • Making judgmental comments
  • Changing the subject
  • Giving false assurance
  • Using gossip and rumors
⚡ Disruptive Interpersonal Behavior +
📌 Types of Disruptive Behavior
  • Incivility: Rude, disrespectful behavior
  • Horizontal Violence: Between nurses at same level
  • Lateral Violence: Similar to horizontal
  • Nurse Bullying: Repeated aggressive behavior
  • Professional Incivility: Unprofessional conduct
✅ Nursing Response
  • Maintain professional demeanor
  • Respond assertively
  • Address issues directly with those involved
  • Seek guidance through nurse-managers
📱 Electronic Communication & Social Media +
⚠️ Critical Reminders
  • ANA and NCSBN have guidelines for RNs using social media
  • Risk for HIPAA violations exists with ANY electronic message
  • Agencies have security measures in place
  • Includes: Social media, email, text messages, telehealth

✅ Teaching Outcomes

📚 Key Teaching Concepts +

Keep education focused on the patient

🚀 Start Early

Begin teaching at the FIRST patient encounter

🎭 Factors Affecting Patient Learning +

🧠 MNEMONIC: "A FCCH" - A Few Caring Concepts Help

AAge & developmental level
FFamily/caregiver support
CCultural influences & Language
CCash (Financial resources)
HHealth literacy
🎓 Knowles' Adult Learning Assumptions +
📌 Four Key Assumptions
  1. As people mature, self-concept moves from dependence → independence
  2. Previous experience is a rich resource for learning
  3. Readiness to learn relates to developmental tasks or social roles
  4. Adults want material to be useful IMMEDIATELY (not in the future)

💡 Test Tip!

Adults want to use what they learn RIGHT NOW - not "someday." This is a common test question!

👴 Teaching Plans for Older Adults +
  • Identify learning barriers first
  • Allow extra time - don't rush!
  • Plan SHORT sessions - attention span
  • Accommodate sensory deficits (vision, hearing)
  • Reduce distractions in environment
  • Relate new info to familiar activities/information
🧠 Three Learning Domains +
⚠️ High-Yield for Exams!
Know these domains AND the teaching strategies for each!
Domain Focus Example Best Strategies
Cognitive Knowledge & recall Patient describes calorie counting Lecture, panel, discovery, written materials
Psychomotor Physical skills Patient demonstrates wound care Demonstration, discovery, printed materials
Affective Attitudes, values, feelings Patient expresses renewed confidence Role modeling, discussion, audiovisual

💡 Remember!

  • Cognitive = thinking (brain knowledge)
  • Psychomotor = doing (physical skills)
  • Affective = feeling (emotions/attitudes)

✅ Promoting Patient Adherence +
📌 Adherence vs Compliance

Adherence is the preferred term over "compliance" because it:

  • Uses a TEAM approach
  • Reflects patient's RIGHT to choose
  • Supports active patient participation
✅ How to Promote Adherence
  • Clear instructions that support patient goals
  • Include patient/caregivers as PARTNERS
  • Use interactive teaching strategies
  • Develop strong interpersonal relationships
📝 Teaching Strategies & Methods +

📚 Materials

  • Audiovisual materials
  • Printed materials
  • Programmed instruction
  • Web-based/technology

🎯 Methods

  • Lecture
  • Discussion
  • Panel discussion
  • Demonstration
  • Discovery
  • Role playing
⚠️ Test Alert
Demonstration is the best strategy for psychomotor learning (like catheter care!)
🏆 Role of the Nurse as Coach +
  1. Establish relationships & identify readiness for change
  2. Identify opportunities, issues, concerns
  3. Establish patient-centered goals
  4. Create structure of coaching interaction
  5. Empower and motivate patients to reach goals
  6. Assist patient to determine progress toward goals
📄 Documentation of Teaching-Learning +
📌 What to Document
  • Summary of the learning need
  • The plan developed
  • Implementation of the plan
  • Evaluation results
💭 Nurse as Counselor +
📌 Counseling Focus Areas
  • Improving coping abilities
  • Reinforcing healthy behaviors
  • Fostering positive interactions
  • Preventing illness and disability

⏱️ Short-term Counseling

For situational crisis (acute events)

📅 Long-term Counseling

For developmental crisis (life stage transitions)

✅ Key Skills

Warmth, friendliness, openness, empathy - CARING is fundamental!

🌍 Culturally Competent Patient Education +
  • Develop understanding of patient's culture
  • Work with multicultural team
  • Be aware of personal assumptions, biases, prejudices
  • Understand core cultural values of patient/group
  • Develop written material in patient's preferred language

👔 Chapter 10: Leading, Managing, and Delegating

Develop leadership skills essential for nursing practice

⚖️ Leadership vs Management +
📌 Covey/Drucker Quote

"Management is doing things right;
Leadership is doing the right things."

👔 Leadership

Ability to direct or motivate a person/group to achieve goals

📋 Management

Planning, organizing, staffing, directing, controlling

Type of Power Description Example
Explicit Power Power by virtue of POSITION Charge nurse, manager
Implied Power Power due to OTHER factors (personality) Class bully, charismatic colleague

💡 Test Tip!

A class bully is an example of IMPLIED power (personality), not explicit (they don't have an official position)!

⭐ Leadership Qualities & Skills +

✨ Qualities

  • Charismatic
  • Dynamic
  • Enthusiastic
  • Poised & confident
  • Self-directed
  • Flexible
  • Knowledgeable
  • Politically aware

🛠️ Skills

  • Commitment to excellence
  • Problem-solving
  • Passion for work
  • Trustworthiness & integrity
  • Respectfulness
  • Accessibility
  • Empathy & caring
  • Promotes staff growth
🎭 Leadership Styles +
Style Characteristics
Autocratic Leader assumes CONTROL; firm, self-assured; center of attention
Democratic Sense of EQUALITY among leader and participants
Laissez-faire Leader RELINQUISHES power to the group; hands-off
Servant Leader serves the team; focuses on others' growth
Transactional Focus on exchanges/transactions; rewards/consequences
Transformational Creates REVOLUTIONARY change; charismatic; inspires vision
Quantum Emphasizes interconnectedness and relationships

⚡ Conflict Management +
📌 Conflict Management vs Engagement
  • Conflict Management: Process to work through conflicts; minimizes negative effects
  • Conflict Engagement: Teaches skills to perform WELL in face of conflict (not avoid it)

Conflict Resolution Strategies:

Strategy Description
Avoiding Ignoring or withdrawing from conflict
Collaborating Working together for win-win solution
Competing Win-lose approach; one party "wins"
Compromising Both parties give up something
Accommodating Giving in to other party's wishes
Smoothing Complimenting all parties to prevent outbursts; focus on agreement

💡 Test Tip!

Smoothing = Complimenting nurses in conflict to prevent emotional outbursts

🔄 Lewin's Theory of Change +

🧠 MNEMONIC: "UMR" - Unfreeze, Move, Refreeze (like ice cream!)

UUnfreezing
Need for change recognized
MMoving
Change initiated after planning
RRefreezing
Change becomes operational
✅ Eight-Step Process for Planned Change
  1. Recognize symptoms, collect data
  2. Identify the problem
  3. Analyze alternative solutions
  4. Select a course of action
  5. Plan for the change
  6. Implement the change
  7. Evaluate effects
  8. Stabilize the change
🛑 Resistance to Change +

❓ Why People Resist

  • Threat to self
  • Lack of understanding
  • Limited tolerance for change
  • Disagreement about benefits
  • Fear of increased responsibility

✅ Overcoming Resistance

  • Explain change in simple language
  • List advantages
  • Relate to existing beliefs/values
  • Provide open communication
  • Indicate how change will be evaluated
  • Introduce change GRADUALLY
  • Provide incentives

📋 ANA Principles for Delegation +
⚠️ RN Cannot Delegate These:
  • Initial patient assessment
  • Discharge planning
  • Health education
  • Care planning
  • Triage
  • Interpretation of patient data
  • Care of invasive lines
  • Administering parenteral medications
✅ RN CAN Delegate:
  • Assistance with basic care activities
  • Collecting vital signs
  • Simple dressing changes
  • Transfers
  • Postmortem care

🧠 5 Considerations When Delegating

SStability of condition
CComplexity of activity
PPotential for harm
PPredictability of outcome
OOverall patient needs
📌 Remember!
RN is RESPONSIBLE and ACCOUNTABLE for nursing practice and supervises ANY AP providing direct patient care.
🩺 Clinical Nurse Leader (CNL) Role +
📌 CNL Overview
  • Created by AACN as a leadership role
  • Works collaboratively with health care team
  • Facilitates, coordinates, oversees patient care
  • Communicates clearly with professionals
  • Integrates evidence-based practices
  • Evaluates patient risks and outcomes
  • Functions as patient advocate, educator, provider
💪 Resilience & Preventing Burnout +

🌟 Developing Resilience

  • Begin/end day with gratitude
  • Practice mindfulness
  • Appreciate human limitations
  • Value connectedness & presence
  • Stretch and deep breathe
  • Reflect on what brings joy
  • Keep positive, hopeful outlook

⚠️ Clinician Burnout

  • Chronic job stress
  • Emotional exhaustion
  • Depersonalization
  • Loss of professional efficacy
  • Threatens quality goals
  • High personal & economic costs
📈 Developing Leadership Responsibilities +
  • Knowledge of administrative structure
  • Mentorship: Experienced nurse guides less experienced
  • Preceptorship: Clinical teaching role
  • Professional organizations: Participate actively
  • Continuing education: Never stop learning
