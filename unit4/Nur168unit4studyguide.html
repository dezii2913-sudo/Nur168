💧 Chapter 41: Fluid, Electrolyte & Acid-Base Balance

Focus: Fluid compartments, electrolyte imbalances, IV solutions, ABG interpretation

💧 Body Fluid Compartments +

Intracellular Fluid (ICF)

70% of body fluid

Fluid INSIDE cells

Extracellular Fluid (ECF)

30% of body fluid

Fluid OUTSIDE cells

• Intravascular (blood vessels)

• Interstitial (between cells)

🚨 HIGH-YIELD: Who Has MORE Body Water?
More Body WaterLess Body Water
Infants (more ECF, prone to deficits!)Older adults
MalesFemales
Lean individualsObese individuals

⭐ TEST TIP

Infants are at HIGHEST risk for fluid volume deficit because they have MORE ECF that can be quickly lost!

⚠️ Fluid Imbalances - CRITICAL +
ImbalanceDefinitionSigns
Hypovolemia Deficiency of water AND electrolytes in ECF ↓BP, ↑HR, poor skin turgor, dark urine, weight loss
Hypervolemia Excessive retention of water AND sodium Edema, ↑BP, distended neck veins, crackles, weight gain
Dehydration Loss of water WITH electrolyte changes Thirst, dry mucous membranes, concentrated urine
Third-Space Shift Fluid shifts to unusable spaces Ascites, edema from burns, peritonitis

🧠 Remember: "HYPO = LOW & DRY, HYPER = HIGH & WET"

LOW
Hypovolemia
HYPO = Running on Empty
Think: "The tank is LOW"
↓BP, ↑HR (heart works harder)
Poor turgor, dark urine, thirsty
💭 "Low and Slow BP, Fast and Furious HR"
HIGH
Hypervolemia
HYPER = Overflowing!
Think: "The tank is overflowing"
Edema, ↑BP, crackles
JVD, weight gain (fluid!)
💭 "So full it's spilling out everywhere"
💡 Clinical Application

Post-op patient: BP dropping, HR increasing, decreased urine output → Think HYPOVOLEMIA

⚡ Electrolytes - Know the BIG 4 +

🧠 "CAT-ions are PAWS-itive!" 🐱

Cations = POSITIVE (Na⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺)

Anions = NEGATIVE (Cl⁻, HCO₃⁻, PO₄³⁻)

Remember: Cats are positive, always landing on their feet! 🐾

ElectrolyteFunctionLow SignsHigh Signs
Na⁺ (Sodium) Controls fluid volume Confusion, seizures Thirst, restlessness
K⁺ (Potassium) Cardiac & cellular function Weakness, flat T waves Peaked T waves, dysrhythmias
Ca²⁺ (Calcium) Nerve, muscle, clotting Tetany, Chvostek's, Trousseau's Lethargy, kidney stones
Mg²⁺ (Magnesium) Enzyme activity Tremors, seizures Lethargy, bradycardia

🧠 Electrolyte Memory Tricks

Na⁺
Sodium
"Salt = Water Magnet"
Where sodium goes, WATER follows!
Controls fluid volume
💭 Think: Eating salty chips makes you THIRSTY and bloated!
K⁺
Potassium
"K = Killer for the heart!"
Both HIGH and LOW K⁺ = deadly dysrhythmias
Too low = flat T's, Too high = peaked T's
💭 "Potassium makes your heart go PUMP or STOP"
Ca²⁺
Calcium
"CATS Crave Calcium"
Chvostek's + Trousseau's = hypoCALCemia
Low Ca²⁺ = twitchy muscles (tetany)
💭 "Tap the face, Pump the cuff = Twitchy stuff"
🚨 POTASSIUM = CARDIAC!

Both hypo AND hyperkalemia cause fatal dysrhythmias!

• Too LOW = flattened T waves

• Too HIGH = peaked T waves → cardiac arrest

⭐ Chvostek's & Trousseau's = HYPOCALCEMIA

Chvostek's: Tap facial nerve → facial twitching

Trousseau's: BP cuff → hand spasm (carpopedal)

🧠 "Check the CHEEK, Test the TUBE (BP cuff)"

💉 IV Solutions - Tonicity +
TypeEffectExamplesUse
Isotonic Stays in vessels 0.9% NS, LR, D5W Volume replacement
Hypotonic Water INTO cells (swell) 0.45% NS Cellular dehydration
Hypertonic Water OUT of cells (shrink) D10W, 3% saline Severe hyponatremia

🧠 "HYPO Hippos SWELL, HYPER Raisins SHRIVEL"

ISO
Isotonic
"ISO = SAME = STAYS"
Same concentration as blood
Fluid stays in vessels
Cells stay the same
💭 0.9% NS, LR = "Normal stays Normal"
🦛
Hypotonic
"HYPO = HIPPO = FAT!"
Less concentrated → water INTO cells
Cells SWELL like a hippo! 🦛
💭 "Hippos are big and swollen - so are HYPO cells!"
🍇
Hypertonic
"HYPER = SHRIVELED raisin"
More concentrated → water OUT of cells
Cells SHRINK like a raisin!
💭 "Grapes become raisins when water leaves!"
🚨 REMEMBER

HYPERTONIC = Cells SHRINK (water leaves cells)

HYPOTONIC = Cells SWELL (water enters cells)

Never give hypotonic to ↑ICP patients (cells will swell MORE!)

⚖️ Acid-Base Balance & ABGs +

🎯 Normal ABG Values - MEMORIZE!

pH: 7.35 - 7.45

CO₂: 35 - 45 mmHg (Respiratory)

HCO₃: 22 - 26 mEq/L (Metabolic)

ImbalancepHCauseExample
Respiratory Acidosis ↓pH, ↑CO₂ Hypoventilation COPD, sedation
Respiratory Alkalosis ↑pH, ↓CO₂ Hyperventilation Anxiety, fever
Metabolic Acidosis ↓pH, ↓HCO₃ Lost bicarb/gained acid DKA, diarrhea
Metabolic Alkalosis ↑pH, ↑HCO₃ Lost acid/gained bicarb Vomiting, NG suction

🏛️ ROME Method - "When in Rome, do as the Romans do!"

R
Respiratory
Respiratory = OPPOSITE
pH and CO₂ go in OPPOSITE directions
↓pH + ↑CO₂ = Resp Acidosis
↑pH + ↓CO₂ = Resp Alkalosis
💭 "Respiratory Rebels - they OPPOSE each other!"
O
Opposite
pH ↔ CO₂ = OPPOSITE
If CO₂ is HIGH, pH is LOW
If CO₂ is LOW, pH is HIGH
They're opposites!
💭 "One goes up, the other goes down"
M
Metabolic
Metabolic = EQUAL
pH and HCO₃ go in SAME direction
↓pH + ↓HCO₃ = Met Acidosis
↑pH + ↑HCO₃ = Met Alkalosis
💭 "Metabolic Mates - they're EQUAL partners!"
E
Equal
pH ↔ HCO₃ = EQUAL
If HCO₃ is LOW, pH is LOW
If HCO₃ is HIGH, pH is HIGH
They move together!
💭 "Best friends go the same direction"

⭐ Quick ABG Steps

1️⃣ Look at pH: <7.35 = Acidosis, >7.45 = Alkalosis

2️⃣ Check CO₂ (35-45): If abnormal & OPPOSITE to pH → Respiratory

3️⃣ Check HCO₃ (22-26): If abnormal & EQUAL to pH → Metabolic

🩸 Blood Transfusions +
🚨 Transfusion Reaction Signs
  • Fever, chills (most common)
  • Back/flank pain
  • Hypotension, tachycardia
  • Dyspnea, wheezing
  • Hives, itching

⭐ IF REACTION OCCURS:

1️⃣ STOP transfusion IMMEDIATELY

2️⃣ Keep IV open with NS

3️⃣ Notify provider

4️⃣ Monitor vital signs

5️⃣ Save blood bag and tubing

💡 Blood Administration Rules
  • Two nurses verify at bedside
  • Use 0.9% NS ONLY - never LR or dextrose
  • Complete within 4 hours
  • Stay with patient first 15 minutes
💉 IV Site Assessment & Complications +

🚨 Infiltration

Definition: IV fluids/meds leak into surrounding tissues instead of bloodstream

Signs:

  • Swelling at site
  • Pain/discomfort
  • Cool skin
  • Pallor/blanching

🔥 Phlebitis

Definition: Inflammation of vein caused by IV therapy

Signs:

  • Redness along vein
  • Warmth at site
  • Pain/tenderness
  • Swelling
💡 Vein Site Selection Factors
  • Accessibility of vein
  • Condition of vein
  • Type of fluid to be infused
  • Anticipated duration of infusion

💧 FLUIDS & ELECTROLYTES - The Body's Plumbing

🏠 THE BIG PICTURE

Think of your body like a house with pipes:

  • ICF (70%) = Water INSIDE the rooms (inside cells)
  • ECF (30%) = Water in the PIPES (outside cells)
  • Babies have MORE water in their pipes - easier to lose!
🚰 TOO LITTLE vs TOO MUCH WATER

📉 HYPOVOLEMIA

NOT ENOUGH water in pipes!

Signs: ↓BP, ↑HR, dry skin, dark pee

Like a garden hose with low pressure

📈 HYPERVOLEMIA

TOO MUCH water in pipes!

Signs: Swelling, ↑BP, crackles in lungs

Like pipes about to burst

⭐ POTASSIUM = HEART!

Too HIGH or too LOW = your heart freaks out! 💓

Low K⁺ = flat T waves (tired heart)

High K⁺ = peaked T waves (angry heart) → EMERGENCY!

💉 IV FLUIDS - CELLS REACT!
SolutionWhat Happens to CellsThink...
HypertonicWater LEAVES → Cells SHRINK🍇 Grape becomes raisin
HypotonicWater ENTERS → Cells SWELL🎈 Balloon filling up
IsotonicNothing happens to cells😊 Just right!

⭐ ROME for ABGs

Respiratory = Opposite

If pH is DOWN ↓ and CO₂ is UP ↑ = Respiratory Acidosis

Metabolic = Equal

If pH is DOWN ↓ and HCO₃ is DOWN ↓ = Metabolic Acidosis
