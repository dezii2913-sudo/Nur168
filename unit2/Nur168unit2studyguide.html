👔 Chapter 10: Leading, Managing, and Delegating

Master leadership styles, delegation principles, and change management

🔄 Leadership vs. Management +

💡 Peter Drucker's Famous Quote!

"Management is doing things RIGHT; Leadership is doing the RIGHT things."

📌 Leadership

The ability to direct or motivate a person or group to achieve set goals. Leaders have power to influence a group which depends on their leadership style.

LeadershipManagement
Directs and motivates toward goalsPlanning, organizing, staffing, directing, controlling
Focuses on vision and directionFocuses on processes and operations
Doing the RIGHT thingsDoing things RIGHT
⚠️ Types of Power - HIGH YIELD!
TypeDefinitionExample
Explicit PowerPower by virtue of POSITIONCharge nurse, nurse manager, CEO
Implied PowerPower due to OTHER FACTORS (personality, intimidation)Class bully, charismatic peer
💡 Test Question Pattern

Q: A class bully who gets his way by intimidating classmates has what type of power?

A: Implied power - No formal position, power comes from personality/intimidation!

⭐ Leadership Qualities & Skills +

🌟 Leadership Qualities

  • Charismatic - Inspires through presence
  • Dynamic - Adapts to rapid changes
  • Enthusiastic - Encourages team energy
  • Poised - Maintains composure under pressure
  • Confident - Makes decisions decisively
  • Self-directed - Manages without constant supervision
  • Flexible - Adjusts to changing needs
  • Knowledgeable - Stays updated with best practices
  • Politically aware - Understands organizational structure

🛠️ Leadership Skills

  • Commitment to excellence
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Passion for one's work
  • Trustworthiness and integrity
  • Respectfulness
  • Accessibility
  • Empathy and caring
  • Responsibility for staff growth
📌 Achieving Self-Knowledge as a Leader
  • Identify your strengths in clinical practice
  • Evaluate how you accomplish work
  • Clarify your values
  • Determine where you belong and what you can contribute
  • Assume responsibility for relationships

⚠️ Overcoming Resistance to Change
  • Explain proposed change to all affected
  • List advantages of the change
  • Relate to person's existing beliefs and values
  • Provide open communication and feedback
  • Indicate how change will be evaluated
  • Introduce change gradually
  • Provide incentives for commitment

A: Smoothing - involves complimenting all parties to prevent emotional outbursts.

💡 Golden Rule of Delegation!

The RN is ALWAYS responsible and accountable for nursing practice. You delegate the TASK, not the RESPONSIBILITY!

🫁 Chapter 40: Oxygenation and Perfusion

📊 Ventilation vs. Respiration vs. Perfusion - MUST KNOW! +

🧠 VRP - Three Processes!

V
Ventilation
Pulmonary Ventilation
Movement of AIR into and out of lungs
"Vent" = moving air like a ventilator!
R
Respiration
Respiration (Gas Exchange)
Exchange of O2/CO2 between alveoli and blood via diffusion
Occurs at alveolar-capillary membrane
P
Perfusion
Perfusion
Oxygenated blood passing through body TISSUES
Blood flow delivering O2 to cells
⚠️ EXACT Test Language!

Q: Pulmonary ventilation is the process by which oxygenated capillary blood passes through body tissues. True or False?

A: FALSE! That's PERFUSION. Pulmonary ventilation is movement of air in/out of lungs!

  • Foreign body obstruction
  • Mucus plugs
  • Airway constriction
  • Immobility (post-surgery!)

🫁 COPD Types

  • Emphysema - Alveolar damage, ↓ lung compliance
  • Chronic Bronchitis - Airway inflammation

⚠️ HIGH YIELD!

The RIGHT mainstem bronchus is shorter, wider, and more vertical - making it the most common site for:

  • Aspiration (food, secretions go to RIGHT lung)
  • ET tube misplacement
  • Right-sided pneumonia

Right = 3 lobes (Right has more Room)

🔴 ALVEOLI - Site of Gas Exchange!
  • ~300 million alveoli in adult lungs
  • Walls = single-cell layer (super thin for diffusion!)
  • Produce SURFACTANT to prevent collapse
⚠️ EXACT Test Question!

Q: Which respiratory organ is the site of gas exchange?

A: Alveoli

Q: Which lung value is the amount of air at maximum inspiration?

A: Total Lung Capacity (TLC)

📊 Pulse Oximetry (SpO2)

  • Normal: 90-100%
  • Chronic lung disease: 88-92% may be normal
  • On O2: target 94-98%
  • Less accurate if SpO2 <80%

📊 Spirometry

  • Measures volume of air exhaled/inhaled
  • Evaluates lung function
  • Promotes deep breathing post-surgery

⚠️ Oxygen Safety Precautions!
  • No open flames in patient's room
  • Post "NO SMOKING" signs
  • Check electrical equipment is in good order
  • Avoid synthetic fabrics (static electricity)
  • Avoid oils near oxygen (ignite spontaneously!)

"A metered-dose inhaler delivers a controlled dose of medication with each compression of the canister."

🫁 Pursed-Lip Breathing

IN through nose, OUT through pursed lips. Prolongs exhalation, improves air trapping. Excellent for COPD.

🫁 Diaphragmatic Breathing

Belly breathing. Abdomen rises on inspiration. More efficient ventilation.

🫁 Incentive Spirometry

Prevents atelectasis. Encourages sustained deep inhalation. Used post-operatively.

✅ Chapter 40 Wrap-Up

📝 Must-Know Points

  • Ventilation = air movement; Respiration = gas exchange; Perfusion = blood through tissues
  • Right bronchus: shorter, wider, vertical → aspiration goes RIGHT
  • Right lung = 3 lobes; Left lung = 2 lobes
  • Alveoli = site of gas exchange; Surfactant prevents collapse
  • Wheezes = continuous, musical (asthma); Stridor = EMERGENCY!
  • TLC = air at maximum inspiration
  • Non-rebreather = HIGHEST O2; Venturi = MOST PRECISE

🧠 Chapter 42: Self-Concept

Understanding identity, self-esteem, and how illness affects self-perception

🧠 Three Dimensions: "KEE"

📌 Important for Nursing!

Identify which area of self-concept is impacted so interventions can be targeted appropriately. When illness disrupts identities (mother, worker, independent person), it significantly affects self-concept.

⚠️ EXACT Test Question!

Q: A nurse asks: "How important is it to you that your work has value to others?" Which aspect is being assessed?

A: Competence - ability to perform job/role in life

📌 Assessing Self-Esteem
  • Significance - Ask about social support and relationships
  • Competence - Ask about abilities and accomplishments
  • Virtue - Ask about values and ethical standards
  • Power - Ask about control and decision-making
😊😔😰 Three Self-Evaluation Feelings +
FeelingBased OnExample
Pride 😊Positive self-evaluation"I'm proud I passed my NCLEX!"
Guilt 😔Behaviors incongruent with IDEAL selfDiabetic patient who ate sugary foods
Shame 😰Low GLOBAL self-worthDeeper sense of inadequacy - "I'm a failure"

💡 Guilt vs. Shame

Guilt = "I DID something bad" (behavior-specific)
Shame = "I AM bad" (global self-worth)

Shame is much more damaging than guilt!

⭐ Self-Actualization +
📌 Self-Actualization

The need to reach one's POTENTIAL through full development of one's unique capability. Highest level of Maslow's hierarchy!

⚠️ EXACT Test Language!

Q: Which term best describes a person's need to reach one's potential by pursuing a career in medicine?

A: Self-actualization (NOT self-esteem!)

📌 Ideal Self

The ideal self constitutes the self one WANTS to be. = TRUE!

👶 Formation of Self-Concept +
⚠️ HIGH YIELD!

People are NOT born with a self-concept! It develops gradually over a lifetime.

StageAgeWhat Happens
Self-awarenessInfancyLearns physical self is different from environment
Self-recognition18 monthsRecognizes self in mirrors and photos
Self-definition3 yearsDescribes self with simple characteristics
Self-concept6-7 yearsStable sense including internal characteristics
📌 Formation Process
  • Infant learns physical self is different from environment
  • If basic needs are met, child has positive feelings of self
  • Child internalizes others' attitudes toward self
  • Child/adult internalizes standards of society
  • In adults, interpersonal losses can impact self-concept
⚠️ False Self

A false self may develop when individuals respond to the needs and ambitions of significant people (like parents) instead of their own.

📋 Factors Affecting Self-Concept +
FactorHow It Affects Self-Concept

📌 Cultural Considerations
  • Cultural modernization - Shift from traditional to modern values
  • Cultural expectations - Shared norms within society
  • Cultural competence - Ability to interact with diverse backgrounds
  • Cultural dissonance - Discomfort when values clash
🩺 Nursing Strategies for Self-Concept +

💪 Identify Personal Strengths

  • Encourage patients to identify positive abilities
  • Replace negative self-talk with balanced thinking
  • Reinforce strengths you observe
  • Foster self-determination - let patients make choices
  • Support through dependency without powerlessness

💜 Maintain Sense of Self

  • Communicate worth with looks, speech, touch
  • Use preferred name; speak respectfully
  • Converse about their life experiences
  • Offer simple explanations for procedures
  • Respect privacy and sensibilities
  • Allow expression of negative feelings
  • Help recognize strengths and explore alternatives
💡 Remember!

Every interaction is an opportunity to support the patient's sense of self. Move the patient's body respectfully, validate emotions, highlight strengths!

✅ Chapter 42 Wrap-Up

📝 Must-Know Points

  • Self-concept: 3 dimensions - Knowledge, Expectation, Evaluation
  • Self-actualization = reaching full POTENTIAL
  • Coopersmith's 4 bases: Significance, Competence, Virtue, Power
  • Competence = ability to perform job/role
  • 3 self-evaluation feelings: Pride, Guilt, Shame
  • Guilt = "I DID bad"; Shame = "I AM bad" (more damaging!)
  • People are NOT born with self-concept - develops over time
  • False self = responding to others' needs instead of own
